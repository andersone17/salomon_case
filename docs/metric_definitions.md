# **Metric Definitions**

## **1. Purpose**

This document defines the primary metrics used to evaluate the Salomon Vestal Pro campaign.

All reporting and analysis should use these definitions consistently.

---

## **2. Revenue Metrics**

### **Gross Revenue**

Total product revenue before discounts, returns, cancellations, taxes, or shipping adjustments.

    Gross Revenue = Product List Price × Units Sold

### **Discount Amount**

The difference between gross revenue and revenue after promotional discounts.

    Discount Amount =
    Gross Revenue
    - Discounted Revenue

### **Net Revenue**

Revenue after discounts, cancellations, and product returns.

    Net Revenue =
    Gross Revenue
    - Discounts
    - Cancellations
    - Returned Revenue

### **Incremental Revenue**

The estimated revenue generated beyond what would likely have occurred without the campaign.

    Incremental Revenue =
    Observed Campaign Revenue
    - Predicted No-Campaign Revenue

### **Attributed Revenue**

Revenue assigned to a marketing channel according to the reporting attribution model.

Attributed revenue is not assumed to equal incremental revenue.

### **Halo Revenue**

Estimated incremental revenue generated across products or categories other than the Vestal Pro because of campaign exposure.

    Halo Revenue =
    Observed Adjacent-Category Revenue
    - Expected Adjacent-Category Revenue Without Campaign

### **Cannibalized Revenue**

Estimated revenue shifted from existing Salomon footwear franchises to the Vestal Pro.

    Cannibalized Revenue =
    Expected Existing-Franchise Revenue
    - Observed Existing-Franchise Revenue

Cannibalized revenue should be estimated only after controlling for inventory, seasonality, promotions, and other relevant factors.

---

## **3. Sales Metrics**

### **Orders**

The number of completed eCommerce transactions.

### **Units Sold**

The total number of product units included in completed transactions.

### **Average Order Value**

Average net revenue generated per completed order.

    Average Order Value =
    Net Revenue
    / Orders

### **Units per Transaction**

Average number of product units included in each order.

    Units per Transaction =
    Units Sold
    / Orders

### **Attach Rate**

The percentage of qualifying footwear orders that also include a product from an adjacent category.

    Attach Rate =
    Footwear Orders Containing an Adjacent Product
    / Total Qualifying Footwear Orders

### **Sell-Through Rate**

The percentage of available inventory sold during a defined period.

    Sell-Through Rate =
    Units Sold
    / Beginning Available Inventory

---

## **4. Traffic and Conversion Metrics**

### **Sessions**

The number of website visits recorded during a reporting period.

### **Users**

The number of distinct website visitors recorded during a reporting period.

### **Product-Detail-Page Views**

The number of views of a product-detail page.

### **Add-to-Cart Rate**

The percentage of product-detail-page sessions that result in an add-to-cart event.

    Add-to-Cart Rate =
    Add-to-Cart Sessions
    / Product-Detail-Page Sessions

### **Checkout Rate**

The percentage of sessions that begin checkout.

    Checkout Rate =
    Checkout Sessions
    / Total Sessions

### **Conversion Rate**

The percentage of sessions resulting in a completed order.

    Conversion Rate =
    Orders
    / Sessions

### **Revenue per Session**

Average net revenue generated per website session.

    Revenue per Session =
    Net Revenue
    / Sessions

### **Bounce Rate**

The percentage of sessions that leave without meaningful additional interaction.

    Bounce Rate =
    Single-Interaction Sessions
    / Total Sessions

---

## **5. Inventory Metrics**

### **Available to Sell**

The quantity of inventory currently available for consumer purchase.

### **In-Stock Rate**

The percentage of product variants with positive available-to-sell inventory.

    In-Stock Rate =
    In-Stock Variants
    / Total Active Variants

### **Core-Size Availability**

The percentage of defined core sizes with positive available-to-sell inventory.

    Core-Size Availability =
    Available Core Sizes
    / Total Core Sizes

### **Size Availability Count**

The number of sizes available for purchase for a product or colorway.

### **Lost-Sales Opportunity**

Estimated sales not captured because desired products, sizes, or colorways were unavailable.

    Estimated Lost Units =
    Expected Units at Healthy Availability
    - Observed Units Sold

Lost-sales estimates should be presented as modeled estimates rather than directly observed outcomes.

---

## **6. Marketing Metrics**

### **Marketing Spend**

Total media or campaign investment associated with a channel.

### **Return on Ad Spend**

Attributed revenue generated for each dollar of marketing spend.

    ROAS =
    Attributed Revenue
    / Marketing Spend

### **Incremental Return on Ad Spend**

Estimated incremental revenue generated for each dollar of marketing spend.

    Incremental ROAS =
    Incremental Revenue
    / Marketing Spend

### **Customer Acquisition Cost**

Average marketing cost required to acquire one new customer.

    Customer Acquisition Cost =
    Marketing Spend
    / New Customers Acquired

### **Cost per Click**

Average media cost for each recorded click.

    Cost per Click =
    Marketing Spend
    / Clicks

### **Click-Through Rate**

The percentage of impressions resulting in a click.

    Click-Through Rate =
    Clicks
    / Impressions

### **Cost per Acquisition**

Average media cost for each attributed conversion or order.

    Cost per Acquisition =
    Marketing Spend
    / Attributed Orders

---

## **7. CRM Metrics**

### **Delivery Rate**

The percentage of sent messages successfully delivered.

    Delivery Rate =
    Delivered Messages
    / Sent Messages

### **Open Rate**

The percentage of delivered emails recorded as opened.

    Open Rate =
    Email Opens
    / Delivered Emails

Open rate should be interpreted cautiously because of privacy-related tracking limitations.

### **Click Rate**

The percentage of delivered messages resulting in a click.

    Click Rate =
    Unique Clicks
    / Delivered Messages

### **Click-to-Open Rate**

The percentage of recorded opens that result in a click.

    Click-to-Open Rate =
    Unique Clicks
    / Unique Opens

### **CRM Conversion Rate**

The percentage of CRM-driven sessions resulting in an order.

    CRM Conversion Rate =
    CRM-Attributed Orders
    / CRM Sessions

### **Revenue per Delivered Message**

Average attributed revenue generated per delivered email or SMS message.

    Revenue per Delivered Message =
    Attributed Revenue
    / Delivered Messages

### **Unsubscribe Rate**

The percentage of delivered messages resulting in an unsubscribe.

    Unsubscribe Rate =
    Unsubscribes
    / Delivered Messages

---

## **8. Customer Metrics**

### **New Customer**

A consumer completing their first recorded purchase through Salomon North America eCommerce.

### **Existing Customer**

A consumer with at least one completed purchase before the current transaction.

### **New-Customer Rate**

The percentage of purchasing customers classified as new customers.

    New-Customer Rate =
    New Purchasing Customers
    / Total Purchasing Customers

### **Repeat-Purchase Rate**

The percentage of customers completing an additional purchase within a defined observation window.

    Repeat-Purchase Rate =
    Customers With a Repeat Purchase
    / Eligible Customers

### **Customer Lifetime Value**

Estimated net contribution generated by a customer across their relationship with the business.

The case study may use an early-value proxy rather than a fully developed lifetime-value model.

### **Database Acquisition**

The number of consumers newly added to the addressable email or SMS database.

### **Email Opt-In Rate**

The percentage of eligible consumers who opt into email communication.

    Email Opt-In Rate =
    New Email Opt-Ins
    / Eligible Consumers

### **SMS Opt-In Rate**

The percentage of eligible consumers who opt into SMS communication.

    SMS Opt-In Rate =
    New SMS Opt-Ins
    / Eligible Consumers

---

## **9. Product and Consumer Experience Metrics**

### **Return Rate**

The percentage of sold units that are returned.

    Return Rate =
    Returned Units
    / Units Sold

### **Net Return Rate**

Returned net revenue divided by original net revenue.

    Net Return Rate =
    Returned Net Revenue
    / Original Net Revenue

### **Consumer-Service Contact Rate**

The percentage of orders associated with at least one Consumer Services contact.

    Consumer-Service Contact Rate =
    Orders With a Consumer-Service Contact
    / Total Orders

### **Sizing-Related Contact Rate**

The percentage of product-related contacts associated with sizing or fit.

    Sizing-Related Contact Rate =
    Sizing and Fit Contacts
    / Total Product-Related Contacts

### **Defect Rate**

The percentage of sold units associated with a confirmed product-defect contact or return.

    Defect Rate =
    Confirmed Defective Units
    / Units Sold

---

## **10. Profitability Metrics**

### **Gross Margin Dollars**

Net revenue remaining after product cost.

    Gross Margin Dollars =
    Net Revenue
    - Cost of Goods Sold

### **Gross Margin Rate**

Gross margin as a percentage of net revenue.

    Gross Margin Rate =
    Gross Margin Dollars
    / Net Revenue

### **Contribution Margin**

Revenue remaining after product cost, returns, variable fulfillment costs, and campaign marketing spend.

    Contribution Margin =
    Net Revenue
    - Cost of Goods Sold
    - Variable Fulfillment Costs
    - Marketing Spend

### **Incremental Contribution Margin**

Estimated contribution margin generated beyond the no-campaign counterfactual.

    Incremental Contribution Margin =
    Incremental Net Revenue
    - Incremental Product Cost
    - Incremental Fulfillment Cost
    - Campaign Marketing Spend

---

## **11. Campaign Evaluation Metrics**

### **Cannibalization Rate**

The estimated share of new-franchise sales shifted from existing Salomon products.

    Cannibalization Rate =
    Estimated Cannibalized Vestal Pro Sales
    / Total Vestal Pro Sales

### **Halo Rate**

The estimated adjacent-category revenue lift relative to the expected no-campaign level.

    Halo Rate =
    Estimated Halo Revenue
    / Expected Adjacent-Category Revenue

### **Net Incremental Campaign Value**

Campaign value after accounting for direct lift, cannibalization, halo revenue, returns, product costs, and marketing spend.

    Net Incremental Campaign Value =
    Direct Incremental Contribution
    - Cannibalized Contribution
    + Halo Contribution
    - Campaign Marketing Spend

---

## **12. Reporting Principles**

- Net revenue should be preferred over gross revenue when evaluating final campaign performance.
- Incremental revenue should be distinguished from attributed revenue.
- Channel performance should include both efficiency and scale.
- Revenue should be evaluated alongside margin, returns, inventory, and consumer experience.
- Cannibalization and halo effects should be estimated rather than assumed.
- Model-based metrics should be reported with uncertainty ranges where practical.
- All comparison periods and denominators should be stated clearly.
