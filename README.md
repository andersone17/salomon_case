## **1. Campaign**

**Campaign:** Salomon Vestal Pro Trail Shoe Launch

**Market:** North America -- ECOM

**Product Overview**: High cushion trail-running shoe designed for ultra running. Designed to provide comfort, traction, and durability across varied surfaces.

### **Campaign Objectives**
#### PRIMARY
- Create incremental footwear revenue.
- Acquire new trail-running consumers.
- Establish the Vestal Pro as a meaningful Salomon footwear franchise.

#### **SECONDARY**
- Increase brand and product awareness.
- Grow Salomon's addressable consumer database through email and SMS acquisition.
- Generate halo demand across trail apparel, accessories, and complementary footwear.
- Limit cannibalization of existing Salomon footwear sales.

---
## **2. Campaign Hypotheses**

### **Primary Hypothesis**

The campaign will generate incremental revenue and new-customer acquisition with minimal cannibalization of existing Salomon footwear sales.

### **Hypothesis Breakdown**

1. **Incremental Revenue**: Observed footwear revenue during the campaign will exceed the estimated revenue that would have occurred without the campaign.

2. **New-Customer Acquisition**: The campaign will attract consumers who have not previously purchased from Salomon's North America eCommerce business.

3. **Halo Effect**: Campaign exposure will create a positive halo effect across adjacent trail-running products, reflected through increased:
   - Site traffic and sessions
   - Product-detail-page (PDP) views on adjacent products
   - Product attach rates to Vestal Pro purchases
   - Units sold and revenue of adjacent products

4. **Cannibalization**: Some sales may shift away from existing Salomon footwear franchises, but the cannibalization rate will be outweighted by the incremental revenue generated by the new franchise.

5. **Inventory Constraints**: Available-to-sell (ATS) inventory will constrain conversion for certain high-demand sizes and colorways, limiting the campaign's full revenue potential.

6. **Channel Efficiency**: CRM will generate more efficient revenue than paid media because it activates existing consumers at a lower cost.

HOWEVER, paid media will contribute more strongly to:
   - Brand reach
   - Site traffic
   - New-customer acquisition
   - Consumer database growth

7. **Customer Value**: Aquiring new customers through the franchise will result in a lifetime-customer-value that is much highter than their initial purchase. This can be predicted based on previous Salomon customer cohorts. We can act on this by targeting customers through CRM channels with complimentary products and offers.

## **6. Definition of Campaign Success**

Campaign success will be evaluated based on **incremental business impact**, rather than attributed revenue alone.

The analysis will distinguish between:
- Direct Vestal Pro revenue
- Estimated incremental revenue
- Cannibalized footwear revenue
- Halo revenue from adjacent products
- Revenue that likely would have occurred without the campaign
- Overall brand awareness

### **Proposed Success Criteria**

| Business Dimension | Success Criterion |
|---|---|
| Incremental Revenue | At least 10% above the estimated no-campaign counterfactual |
| Revenue Plan | Meet or exceed the launch-period net-revenue target |
| New-Customer Acquisition | At least 25% of Vestal Pro purchasers are new customers |
| Cannibalization | Less than 25% of Vestal Pro sales are estimated to have shifted from existing footwear franchises |
| Halo Effect | At least 5% incremental lift across adjacent trail categories |
| Marketing Efficiency | Incremental contribution margin exceeds campaign marketing cost (CM3, ROAS, MER) |
| Consumer Database | Email and SMS acquisition meets or exceeds campaign targets |
| Consumer Experience | Return and service-contact rates remain near historical category benchmarks |
| Inventory Availability | Core sizes maintain sufficient availability to support campaign demand |

Revenue growth alone will not determine whether the campaign was successful.

We need to also monitor:
- Gross margin
- Contribution margin (CM3)
- Discount rate (Brand perception)
- Return rate
- Customer acquisition cost
- Marketing return on ad spend
- Incremental return on ad spend
- Available-to-sell inventory
- In-stock rate
- Consumer-service contact rate
- Email unsubscribe rate
- SMS unsubscribe rate
- Existing-franchise sales
- Site conversion rate
- Product-detail-page conversion rate
- Repeat-purchase rate

---

## **8. Evaluation Principle**

> Campaign performance will be evaluated using estimated incremental business impact rather than channel-attributed revenue alone. The analysis will separate direct franchise sales, cannibalized sales, halo revenue, and the estimated no-campaign baseline.
